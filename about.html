Marisa is Seattle native and former Social Justice Educator with 10+ years of teaching and facilitation experience, looking to leverage their leadership, resilience and creativity as a full stack developer.

Before applying to Ada, Marisa taught pretty much everything; from swimming and sewing, to 7 years as a social justice centered elementary teacher. She loved the creative challenge of teaching, but found herself frustrated by the instability. The annual rotation of school leadership and teaching staff, not to mention deep seeded structures of oppression inherent in education, undermined her efforts to enact lasting change and stability her students desperately needed. The programmers in Marisa’s life tempted her to join them with the idea of developing products that schools, businesses, everyone, could rely on for more than 9 months.

Through Ada Developers Academy, Marisa discovered the thrill and joy of developing clean, creative code solutions in the company of other brillant aspiring programmers. They continue to utilize their facilitation and organizing experience. So far they have organized a regular study group, as well as Gaydies: a group for queer identified Ada students to connect and support one another.

Marisa looks forward to continuing to expand their technical and practical knowledge as well as organizing space for marginalized communities in tech.
